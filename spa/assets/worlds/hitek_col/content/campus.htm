Probably the best way to go about implementing this would be to install the campus as one of our colonies, which traditionally provide housing and themed content to residents.

The Campus Colony would do all of these things, and more.  It would exist both in 2D and 3DVR.

In 3D, we would have a main campus, where users can walk up and down the streets and walk by various buildings and campus landmarks and educational facilities.

It would probably be best to divide the colony into separate schools.  Therefore in Cybertown terminology, we could have a Science Neighborhood, Engineering Neighborhood, etc.  If you are not familiar with our residential structure, neighborhoods divide up the major colony theme into more in-depth sections.  Our engineering neighborhood would simply represent the part of campus with the engineering facilities and student housing.

Neighborhoods could contain their own 3D worlds, just as the colony itself does.  One possibility is to make the interiors of certain buildings of the main campus world act as worlds for the different neighborhoods.  Thus, as a citizen walks around the main campus, they can enter the Engineering Building and would then have access to the 3D interior and the 2D content and resources that would be provided by the Engineering Neighborhood and could go on from there.

Today, Cybertown neighborhoods are divided up into city blocks that contain housing space for residents.  To create a more immersive and useful environment, we have done some research into creating city blocks for purposes other than housing.

What we would like to do for the Campus is to create blocks for traditional housing purposes, as well as to accommodate other features such as individual educational buildings and commercial areas and shops that can be purchased or leased by other sponsors.  Below is an incomplete, and lower quality testing version of a neighborhood map that shows how blocks of the future may be assembled:

Residential blocks would provide spaces for students to build their houses and be close by to the educational content they need. They would be exactly like the blocks currently available in Cybertown today.  Instructional buildings like the Electrical Engineering Building (shown above) could possibly contain a 3D world and other resources that would be applicable.

In this type of system, Universities that you serve could possibly have multiple blocks and locations around the campus.  For example, the University of Phoenix could have buildings and housing areas in multiple neighborhoods, rather than have a neighborhood of their own.  By dividing the colony in this fashion, users can be exposed to the many different options available to them for each particular field of study.

Many resources, including links, tutorials, information, and even advertisements can be made accessible via the standard 2d utilities that are integrated into the colonies.  Custom links can also be integrated to provide easy access to the websites that we are wanting them to find and visit!

Customized interfaces in 2D can also be installed to give the colony a unique and well integrated look. 

If we want to add some extra content, we could add buildings that offer free training that citizens may find of interest.  Popular choices may be skills and technologies that can help them in Cybertown, including:

  • HTML/Web Development
  • VRML, World Building and Architecture
  • Imaging
  • Macromedia Flash Development

One of our upcoming neighborhoods in the Hi-Tek Colony, Origins, will offer tutorials and information about the fundamentals of a high-tech career as well as some basic learning materials for fields such as electrical engineering and computer science.  Some examples of possible resources are:

  • Tutorials in various levels of Math, Chemistry, Physics, Biology
  • Tutorials and walkthroughs of fun projects that deal with various fields, including Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Computer Technology, or anything that other skilled citizens can contribute.
